04The Programs  ·  Discourse  ·  Field Response
קריאה לפעולה
Call to Action
Initiative launched in the immediate aftermath of October 7, in response to sweeping anti-Israel sentiment in the world of professional dance, and professional discrimination and injury toward Israeli dance artists, creators, dancers, and researchers abroad. A coalition of dance professionals and institutions was formed to offer space for dialogue, thinking and working groups, touring and safety protocols, documentation of individual cases, legal consultation on discriminatory contract breaches, and referrals to trusted professionals.
Over 150 Israeli artists and dance professionals joined the online forums.
שיחות על מחול [ישראלי]
Conversations on [Israeli] Dance
Ongoing platform for discourse — public and private events that bring local and international artists, thinkers, and cultural figures into conversation around artistic practice and the realities of working in complex and shifting field.
First Series · Where We Stand Now
איפה אנחנו עומדות עכשיו — שיחות על מחול [ישראלי] אחרי ה־7 באוקטובר
Almost three years into an altered landscape for Israeli artists — broken relationships, cancelled engagements, boycott, censorship, deepening isolation. A protected space to ask not only what is happening around us, but what we've learned from rupture, where we hold agency, and what repair or re-imagination is possible now.
180 choreographers, dancers, artistic and festival directors, and scholars — Israeli and international, from 15 countries.

Field Notes
01Sarah Holcman · Co-Founder · Artistic and Executive Director
Dance artist, artistic director, curator, and producer with over 20 years in the field.
Previous roles include: Gibney Dance Center, Movement Research, and the 92nd Street Y (New York) — curating and managing residencies, works-in-progress series, and artist resource programs; and Hollins University/American Dance Festival managing the Masters of Fine Arts in Dance program.
2015–2022 · Director of Programs and International Relations, Suzanne Dellal Centre for Dance — overseeing international curation, directing International Exposure, and initiating the Centre's first choreographic development programs and residencies.
02Tamar Sonn · Co-Founder
Dancer, choreographer assistant, dance community activist and initiator.
Tamar’s career spans performance, collaboration, and organizing. She has performed in the works of a wide range of companies and choreographers and since 2019 she has been a close collaborator and assistant to Berlin-based choreographer Kat Válastur. In 2020, she worked with Nitsan Margaliot to initiate The Platform for Independent Dancers in Israel, building sustainable conditions for freelance dancers in the field.
03Nitsan Margaliot · Co-Founder
Choreographer, curator, performer, researcher, dance community organizer.
Nitsan’s work spans performance, archival practice, artistic research, and organizational initiative. He danced with the Batsheva Ensemble before building an international career with works presented across Europe and the U.S. He has initiated an alter-archive project, co-curated festivals and exhibitions, and in 2020 he initiated The Platform for Independent Dancers in Israel with Tamar Sonn.
07The New Program · 2027
גוף עבודה
Body of Work
A pilot program of long-term choreographic research residencies for professional choreographers and dancers. Four working groups, each composed of one choreographer, 5–15 dancers, and mentors from the fields of dramaturgy, writing, and dance research. Each group takes part in a two-month studio residency of artistic research with no expectation of a final product — developing new or existing choreographic material, repertory, improvisation, and performance practices.
